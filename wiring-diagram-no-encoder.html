⚡ WIRING NOTES

  • Motor direction: If a motor spins the wrong way, simply swap its M+ and M− wires at the DRV8833 output — no code change needed.
  • DRV8833 SLP pin: Must be tied HIGH (to VCC/3V3) to enable the driver. Left floating = motors won't move.
  • VM (motor power): Connect directly to your battery positive. Do not rely on the ESP32's 5V/VIN pin to power motors under load.
  • Common GND: ESP32, both DRV8833 boards, servo, and battery negative must all share one common GND.
  • Servo power: Small servos can run from 3V3. For larger/high-torque servos, power from a dedicated 5V BEC and share its GND with the ESP32.
  • Decoupling caps: Place a 100µF electrolytic + 100nF ceramic cap across VM and GND close to each DRV8833 to suppress motor switching noise.
  • GPIO 48 (RGB): Onboard WS2812 NeoPixel — no external wiring needed.
  • Encoder code: Your sketch still initialises the encoders — they'll just always read 0 with plain motors. You can safely remove those lines if desired.
