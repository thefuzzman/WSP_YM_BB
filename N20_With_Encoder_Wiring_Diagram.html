⚡ IMPORTANT WIRING NOTES

  • DRV8833 SLP pin: Must be tied HIGH (to VCC/3V3) to enable the driver. If left floating, motors won't run.
  • VM (Motor Power): Connect to your battery positive. The DRV8833 supports 2.7V–10.8V — match your N20 motor voltage (typically 3V, 5V, or 6V). Do NOT use the ESP32 5V pin for motors under load.
  • VCC (Logic): Connect to ESP32 3V3. This powers the DRV8833 logic separately from the motor supply.
  • Servo power: Small servos (< 500mA) may run from 3V3 if your regulator can handle it. For larger servos, power from a separate 5V BEC/regulator, sharing GND with the ESP32.
  • N20 Encoder power: The encoder inside the N20 requires its own 3V3 and GND connections (in addition to the signal wires). Check your motor's datasheet for exact pinout — C1/C2 are the quadrature signals, VCC/GND power the Hall sensor.
  • Common GND: All grounds (ESP32, both DRV8833 boards, motors, encoders, servo, battery) MUST share a common GND reference.
  • Decoupling caps: Add a 100µF electrolytic + 100nF ceramic capacitor across VM and GND near each DRV8833 to suppress motor switching noise.
  • GPIO 48 (RGB LED): This is the onboard WS2812 NeoPixel on the ESP32-S3-WROOM-1 — no external wiring needed.
  • Each DRV8833 drives one motor (using the A-channel only). BOUT1/BOUT2 are unused — leave them open or use a second motor if desired.
